Worldview Collision Timeline

Tracing the historical collision of colonial policy and Indigenous resilience.

1492

Terra Nullius

The Doctrine of Discovery declares Indigenous lands "empty" and available for claim by Christian monarchs, initiating centuries of dispossession.

1763

Royal Proclamation

Recognizes Aboriginal title and establishes the protocol for treaty-making, acknowledging Indigenous nations as distinct political entities.

1876

The Indian Act

Consolidated colonial control over Indigenous identity, governance, and land. Imposed the reserve system and banned cultural practices like the Potlatch.

1927

Legal Counsel Ban

Amendment to the Indian Act makes it illegal for First Nations to hire lawyers to pursue land claims, effectively silencing legal resistance for decades.

1973

Calder Case

Supreme Court of Canada acknowledges that Aboriginal title existed prior to colonization and was not extinguished by it, opening the door for modern land claims.

1996

Last Residential School Closes

The closure of the Gordon Residential School in Saskatchewan marks the end of the state-run school system designed to assimilate Indigenous children.

2015

TRC Final Report

The Truth and Reconciliation Commission releases 94 Calls to Action, framing reconciliation as a process of healing relationships and addressing historical wrongs.

2018

Rise of IPCAs

Indigenous Protected and Conserved Areas (IPCAs) gain momentum, asserting Indigenous law and governance over traditional territories (e.g., Edéhzhíe).

Present

Two-Eyed Seeing

A growing movement to integrate Indigenous and Western knowledge systems in science, law, and land management for a sustainable future.

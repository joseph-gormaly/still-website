About STILL

STILL is a research initiative focused on developing evidence-based policy frameworks for Indigenous-led land management. We specialize in IPCA governance, GIS analysis, and resource valuation methodologies.

Our work bridges academic research with practical implementation, providing measurable outcomes for sustainable land use.

Our Approach

STILL brings together Indigenous knowledge keepers and Western scientists to create holistic approaches to land stewardship. We practice Etuaptmumk (Two-Eyed Seeing)—learning to see from one eye with the strengths of Indigenous ways of knowing, and from the other eye with the strengths of Western ways of knowing, and using both eyes together.

This approach allows us to develop solutions that are both scientifically rigorous and culturally grounded.

Our Values

"We walk with the understanding that land is not a resource to be extracted, but a relative to be honoured. Our work grows from listening—to Elders, to the waters, to the stories written in stone and soil."

We carry the responsibility passed down through generations: to care for the land so that it may care for those yet to come. All our relations.
